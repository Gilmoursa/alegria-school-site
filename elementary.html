Montessori Elementary Program



Elementary Years

The Alegria Montessori elementary classroom focuses on four major components: basic academics (meeting Montessori and state guidelines ), The Great Lessons, “going out”, and community service. Our academic standards are set with the high expectations of our teachers to assist your child in understanding the concepts of learning through the materials we provide, and then into abstraction. The Great Lessons are stories that Montessori developed in order to generate a child’s interest in science, history, and culture.

Our “going out” concept helps your child understand community resources and how to use them by researching topics, for example cultures and languages, that they learn each semester. This allows them to discover the topics discussed in class in depth, further expanding their need and desire to want to know more about the world around them. Our last concept, community service, is exactly that. We help encourage your children to find a community project, to research that project, and then to follow through with active involvement.

Lower Elementary – Grades 1-3

The elementary teaching team of Carolina, Cyndi, and Kristen should make for an amazing experience for our students.

Stephanie Mussett
Elementary Coordinator

Stephanie was born and raised in Cincinnati, Ohio, where she attended public Montessori through high school. She moved to St. Petersburg when she became a student at Eckerd College. After graduation, Stephanie received her Lower Elementary certification from Seacoast Center for Education while establishing the elementary program at Montessori by the Sea on St. Pete Beach.

Stephanie then worked at Country Day School in Largo for three years coordinating curriculum and teaching Lower Elementary. She started searching for a school closer to home after having her son, Zachary, and was thrilled to discover the wonderful community of Alegria.

Stephanie has completed her Master of Education through Plymouth State University and is currently finishing her Administration Credential from Houston Montessori Center. She lives with her husband, Mark, Zachary, two dachshunds, and two birds in Pinellas Point. Their tortoise, Tortito, currently resides in the Upper Elementary classroom.

Jennifer Lindley
Elementary – Grades 1 – 6

Jennifer is a fourth-generation Florida native, wife, and mother of two who earned her Bachelor of Science degree from Eckerd College in St. Petersburg, FL majoring in marine science and biology. After many successful years in finance and clinical research fields, she discovered her love of teaching and has spent the last several years at a gifted academy in Tampa teaching science, social studies, and language arts at the elementary level, and finance, marine science, and psychology at the high school level. Jennifer enjoys coordinating nature hikes, gardening, watching her kids play competitive soccer, and staying active as a volunteer for local community service projects with her family. She is currently enrolled in the Montessori Elementary Teacher Training Collaborative for the Elementary I-II (6-12) program. Both of Jennifer’s children attended Alegria from ages 2-5 and they are excited to be a part of the Alegria family again.

Michelle Chaignon-Hatch
