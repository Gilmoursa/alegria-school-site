Alegria Montessori



School Administration

The Alegria Montessori elementary classroom focuses on four major components: basic academics (meeting Montessori and state guidelines ), The Great Lessons, “going out”, and community service. Our academic standards are set with the high expectations of our teachers to assist your child in understanding the concepts of learning through the materials we provide, and then into abstraction. The Great Lessons are stories that Montessori developed in order to generate a child’s interest in science, history, and culture.

Our “going out” concept helps your child understand community resources and how to use them by researching topics, for example cultures and languages, that they learn each semester. This allows them to discover the topics discussed in class in depth, further expanding their need and desire to want to know more about the world around them. Our last concept, community service, is exactly that. We help encourage your children to find a community project, to research that project, and then to follow through with active involvement.

Sara Madle
Montessori Director

Sara Madle is the founder and director of Alegria Montessori School. She has 20 years of teaching experience spanning Montessori and English as a Second Language (ESOL), children and adults, private and public schools, from D.C. to Hawaii, and finally to St. Petersburg. Sara has a BA in Spanish/Latin American Studies from American Univ. in Washington DC. along with her Preprimary 3-6 Montessori certificate from Chaminade Univ. Sara later completed her ESOL endorsement and MA in Reading through the University of South Florida. Finally, Sara was happy to return to Montessori and completed her EdS degree in Montessori Elem. with Barry Univ.

Sara is married to Ali, who is from England, and has 3 wonderful children. Austin,thank you for a great website, Kiana who is the best nurse in the world according to Sophie, and Sophie who attends Alegria but is already a Gator at heart.

Sara feels very fortunate that at Alegria she has been able to combine her love of Montessori, with the children of St. Petersburg, and frequent visits from international students. One goal for the school is to develop its bilingual program and to continue to grow the elementary program.

Tanya Rotter
Alegria Lakewood Campus Director/Toddler Teacher

Tanya Rotter taught in a traditional preschool in Kentucky for 8 years before moving to Florida, working with children from 1 year to 12 years of age. She explored other career options in retail management before returning to her real love – teaching! Tanya is the lead teacher and site director at our Alegria Lakewood campus. She will also coordinate the Parent/Infant Education classes . Tanya attended Eckerd College, has her Child Development Associate, and has finally done her 0 – 3 Montessori training with Palm Harbor Teacher Education Center!
